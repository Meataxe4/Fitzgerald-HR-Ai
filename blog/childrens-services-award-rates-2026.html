Children's Services Award Rates 2026 — Pay & Penalties

1 Jul 2026 Updated 1 Jul 2026 By Fitz HR 6 min read Reviewed against FWO Pay Guide MA000120

The Children's Services Award MA000120 covers long day care, preschools, kindergartens and outside-school-hours care. Between classification levels, the educational leader allowance and broken shifts, there is plenty to get wrong. This guide sets out the 2026 rates.

Under the Children's Services Award MA000120 (2026), a Support Worker Level 1.1 earns $26.44/hr full-time. Saturday is 150% (175% casual), Sunday 200% (225% casual) and public holidays 250% (275% casual). An educational leader appointed 5+ days a week is paid an allowance of $4,784.28 per year, and a broken shift attracts $21.38 per day. The 25% casual loading is already built into the casual penalty percentages.

Rates current as at 2026-07-01 (Annual Wage Review), sourced from the Fair Work Ombudsman Pay Guide MA000120. Next review 2027-07-01.

Penalty Rates & Shift Loadings

Weekend and public holiday penalties are a percentage of the ordinary hourly rate; casual rates are all-inclusive of the 25% loading:

When workedFull-time / part-timeCasual (all-inclusive)
Saturday150%175%
Sunday200%225%
Public holiday250%275%

Shift loadings apply for early-morning (10%), afternoon (15%), rotating night (17.5%) and permanent night (30%) work.

Educational Leader & Broken Shifts

An educational leader appointed 5 or more days a week is paid an allowance of $4,784.28 per year (a pro-rata amount applies for fewer days). A broken shift attracts $21.38 for each day one is worked. See our educational leader guide and broken shift guide.

Common Mistakes That Trigger Audits

✗
Paying educator work at support-worker rates. The classification turns on the work performed, not the roster label.
✗
Forgetting the educational leader allowance. An appointed educational leader is owed the allowance — $4,784.28/year for 5+ days.
✗
Missing the broken-shift allowance. $21.38 for each day a broken shift is worked.
✗
Paying Sunday rates on public holidays. Public holidays are 250%/275%, not 200%/225%.
