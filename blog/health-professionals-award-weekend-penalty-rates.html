Health Professionals Award Weekend Penalty Rates

1 Jul 2026 Updated 1 Jul 2026 By Fitz HR 5 min read Reviewed against FWO Pay Guide MA000027

Weekend and after-hours work is common in private health. This guide sets out the exact Health Professionals Award MA000027 penalty rates for 2026 and how the shift loading and overtime fit around them.

Under MA000027, Saturday and Sunday are both 150% (175% casual) and public holidays 250% (275% casual). Casual percentages already include the 25% loading. A 15% shift loading applies to defined Monday–Friday shifts, and overtime is 150% for the first 2 hours and 200% after.

Rates current as at 2026-07-01 (Annual Wage Review), sourced from the Fair Work Ombudsman Pay Guide MA000027. Next review 2027-07-01.

Health Penalty Rates 2026

Saturday: 150% (FT/PT) · 175% (casual)
Sunday: 150% (FT/PT) · 175% (casual)
Public holiday: 250% (FT/PT) · 275% (casual)
Shift loading (Mon–Fri): +15%
Overtime: 150% (first 2 hrs) then 200%

Worked Examples

For a Support Services Level 1 employee on $26.97/hr:

  • Saturday: $40.46/hr (FT/PT) · $47.20/hr (casual)
  • Sunday: $40.46/hr (FT/PT) · $47.20/hr (casual)
  • Public holiday: $67.43/hr (FT/PT) · $74.17/hr (casual)

Note that Saturday and Sunday are the same rate under MA000027 — unlike many awards where Sunday is higher.

Shift Loading & Overtime

A 15% shift loading applies to defined Monday–Friday shifts (for example, shifts finishing late). Overtime is 150% for the first 2 hours and 200% after. The shift loading is not paid on top of a weekend penalty or overtime for the same hour — the higher single entitlement applies.

Common Mistakes That Trigger Audits

✗
Assuming Sunday is higher than Saturday. Under MA000027 they are the same (150%/175%).
✗
Paying Sunday rates on public holidays. Public holidays are 250%/275%.
✗
Stacking the shift loading on penalties or overtime. The higher single entitlement applies, not both.
