Health Professionals Award Rates 2026 — Pay & Penalties

1 Jul 2026 Updated 1 Jul 2026 By Fitz HR 6 min read Reviewed against FWO Pay Guide MA000027

The Health Professionals and Support Services Award MA000027 covers a wide private-sector workforce — allied health, dental assistants, pathology collectors and administration — across several streams. This guide sets out the 2026 rates and the rules behind them.

Under the Health Professionals Award MA000027 (2026), a Support Services Level 1 employee earns $26.97/hr full-time. Saturday and Sunday are both 150% (175% casual) and public holidays 250% (275% casual). A 15% shift loading applies to certain Monday–Friday shifts. The casual minimum engagement is 3 hours, and the casual loading of 25% is already built into the casual penalty percentages.

Penalty Rates & Shift Loading

Weekend and public holiday penalties are a percentage of the minimum hourly rate; casual rates are all-inclusive of the 25% loading:

When workedFull-time / part-timeCasual (all-inclusive)
Saturday150%175%
Sunday150%175%
Public holiday250%275%

A 15% shift loading applies to defined Monday–Friday shifts. Overtime is 150% for the first 2 hours and 200% after.

Casual Loading & Minimum Engagement

Casual employees receive a 25% loading and a minimum engagement of 3 hours per shift. Many practices also use annualised wage arrangements for full-time professionals — see our annualised wage guide for the strict conditions.

Common Mistakes That Trigger Audits

✗
Using the wrong stream. A dental assistant, pathology collector and health professional sit in different streams with different rates.
✗
Treating a salary as covering everything. Annualised wages have strict conditions and a mandatory reconciliation — see our annualised wage guide.
✗
Rostering casuals under 3 hours. The casual minimum engagement is 3 hours.
✗
Paying Sunday rates on public holidays. Public holidays are 250%/275%.
