What to Look For When Choosing

Most venue owners make the wrong choice because they evaluate HR software on features — rostering, dashboards, integrations — rather than on compliance depth. HR problems in hospitality don’t come from missing a dashboard. They come from incorrect decisions made under pressure: a dismissal handled wrong, a warning letter that doesn’t hold up, a casual conversion obligation missed for 12 months.

  • Hospitality Award specificity — does the platform know the difference between a Level 2 and Level 4 classification? Does it calculate split shift allowances correctly? If not, it’s a general tool with hospitality branding.
  • After-hours availability — hospitality HR crises happen at 10pm Saturday, not 9am Tuesday. What happens when you need guidance outside business hours?
  • Document defensibility — can it generate a warning letter that references the correct Award clause and follows procedural fairness? The cost of getting this wrong is significant — see our guide on unfair dismissal costs for Australian hospitality. Or does it produce a generic template?
  • Pricing transparency — can you see the price before speaking to sales? Lock-in contracts and renewal price increases are common in this category. For context on the cost of non-compliance, see our Fair Work fines guide and PIP template for hospitality.
  • Genuine expert access — when AI guidance isn’t enough, is a qualified HR professional available? At what cost and how quickly?
