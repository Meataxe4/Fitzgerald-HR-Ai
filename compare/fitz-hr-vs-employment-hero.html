Fitz HR vs Employment Hero: Which Is Better for Hospitality?

13 Mar 2026 · By Fitz HR · 7 min read · Comparison Employment Hero

Employment Hero is one of Australia's largest HR platforms. Fitz HR is a specialist platform built exclusively for hospitality. Both serve Australian businesses, but they take very different approaches. This comparison helps you decide which is right for your venue.

Where Fitz HR Wins

Hospitality-specific depth: Fitz HR's AI is trained specifically on the Hospitality Industry (General) Award and the Restaurant Industry Award. When you ask a question about penalty rates, casual conversion, or split shift allowances, you get an answer that references the correct clause in the correct Award. Employment Hero covers Australian workplace law broadly but does not have the same depth on hospitality-specific issues.

AI-powered document generation: Fitz HR generates customised documents based on your specific situation, not generic templates. You answer questions about the issue, and the AI produces a warning letter, PIP, or contract that references the correct Award provisions and follows procedural fairness.

Expert human review: Every document generated by Fitz HR can be reviewed by a qualified HR consultant. Employment Hero does not offer this.

Crisis Mode: When a serious staff situation occurs outside business hours, Fitz HR connects you with a qualified HR professional. Employment Hero does not have an equivalent feature.

Where Employment Hero Wins

Full payroll: Employment Hero includes comprehensive payroll with STP reporting. Fitz HR does not process payroll — it integrates with Xero and MYOB.

Employee benefits platform: Employment Hero Swag offers employee discounts and perks. Fitz HR focuses on compliance and people management, not benefits.

Scale: For larger hospitality groups with 50+ employees across multiple venues, Employment Hero's enterprise features may be more appropriate.

The Verdict

If you run a single hospitality venue and your primary concern is HR compliance, getting Award questions answered, and generating proper documentation, Fitz HR is the better choice. It is purpose-built for your industry, more affordable, and deeper on hospitality-specific issues. If you need a full HRIS with payroll, benefits, and enterprise-scale features, Employment Hero is the more comprehensive platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Fitz HR better than Employment Hero for hospitality?

For hospitality-specific HR compliance, yes. Fitz HR is purpose-built for the Hospitality Industry (General) Award, provides AI-generated documents specific to your situation, and includes expert human review and Crisis Mode. Employment Hero is a broader platform better suited to larger multi-industry businesses that need full payroll and benefits.

Does Fitz HR do payroll?

No. Fitz HR focuses on HR compliance, guidance, and document generation. For payroll, it integrates with Xero and MYOB. This is by design — payroll platforms like Xero already do payroll well, so Fitz HR focuses on the HR compliance gap that payroll platforms don't cover.

Which is cheaper, Fitz HR or Employment Hero?

Fitz HR starts at $0 (free tier) with paid plans from $249/year. Employment Hero starts at approximately $8/employee/month. For a venue with 15 employees, Employment Hero would cost approximately $1,440/year compared to Fitz HR's $249-$449/year.
