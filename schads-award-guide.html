SCHADS Award Guide

The complete reference to the Social, Community, Home Care and Disability Services Industry Award MA000100 for NDIS providers, community services, home care and disability support organisations — coverage, classifications, penalty rates, allowances, minimum engagement and Fair Work compliance.

Quick answer

The SCHADS Award (MA000100) sets minimum pay by classification, starting at $27.55/hour full-time (Social and community services - Level 1 - pay point 1 (social community services)). Saturdays pay 150% (full/part-time) or 175% (casual); Sundays 200% (full/part-time) or 225% (casual); public holidays 250% (full/part-time) or 275% (casual). Casual percentages already include the 25% casual loading. Figures current as at 1 July 2026, sourced from the Fair Work Ombudsman Pay Guide — full tables below, or ask Fitz for the exact rate for your classification and shift.

What the SCHADS Award Covers

The Social, Community, Home Care and Disability Services Industry Award MA000100 covers social and community services, home care, disability services (including NDIS), family day care and crisis accommodation. Getting coverage right matters — it drives the classification structure, penalty rates, and allowances that apply to every shift. Applying the wrong award creates systematic underpayment exposure.

Minimum Engagement

The SCHADS Award sets a minimum period an employee must be paid for each time they are engaged:

  • social community services hours per shift: 3 hours minimum per shift
  • other streams hours per shift: 2 hours minimum per shift
  • part time hours per shift: 2 hours minimum per shift
  • casual hours per shift: 2 hours minimum per shift

Fair Work Compliance

Operating under MA000100 creates obligations beyond paying the correct rate:

  • Record keeping: keep employee records for 7 years, including actual (not rostered) start and finish times, classification, rates, allowances and superannuation.
  • Payslips: issue within one working day of pay day with all required information.
  • Casual Employment Information Statement: provide at commencement and at the relevant conversion milestone.
  • Superannuation: currently 12% on ordinary time earnings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Social, Community, Home Care and Disability Services Industry Award MA000100?
It is a modern award under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth) that sets minimum pay rates, penalty rates, allowances and conditions. It covers social and community services, home care, disability services (including NDIS), family day care and crisis accommodation.
What are the penalty rates under MA000100 in 2026?
Saturday 150% and Sunday 200% for full-time and part-time employees, with a public holiday rate of 250%. Casual rates are all-inclusive of the 25% loading. See the full pay rates page.
Does the SCHADS Award cover NDIS support workers?
Yes. The Social, Community, Home Care and Disability Services Industry Award MA000100 covers most disability support and NDIS-funded services, alongside home care, family day care and crisis accommodation streams — each with its own classification and minimum-engagement rules.
How current are these SCHADS Award figures?
They are extracted from the Fair Work Ombudsman Pay Guide MA000100, current as at 2026-07-01, with the next review due 2027-07-01.
