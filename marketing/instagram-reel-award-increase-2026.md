## Format

- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (1080 × 1920 px) — full-bleed backgrounds, no black bars
- **Safe zone:** all text/logos kept inside the **1010 × 1440** centred safe area (x: 35–1045, y: 240–1680) so Instagram's top bar, caption and right-hand action rail never cover them. Verify with the "Safe zone" toggle in `reel-cards/reel-animated.html` or `reel-cards/preview/_safezone-check.png`.
- **Duration:** 30–40 seconds (≈6s per tip + intro + CTA)
- **Pacing:** One tip per card, hard cut or quick slide on the beat
- **Sound:** Trending upbeat/business audio under a voiceover (VO optional — captions carry it)
- **Captions:** Burned-in (auto-captions ON) — most viewers watch muted
- **Placement:** Reels + Stories (single vertical export works for both)

### Card 1 — Tip 1 (0:03–0:09)
- **Badge:** `1`
- **Title:** It starts on your **first full pay period** — not 1 July.
- **Body:** The 4.75% rise applies from the **first full pay period on or after 1 July 2026**. If your pay week starts mid-week, you keep paying the old rate until the next period begins. Paying early *or* late both create errors.
- **Accent stat:** `Effective: 1st full pay period ≥ 1 Jul 2026`
- **VO:** "Tip one — it does not start on the first of July. It starts on your first full pay period on or after the first. Pay early and you've overpaid, late and you've underpaid."

### Card 2 — Tip 2 (0:09–0:15)
- **Badge:** `2`
- **Title:** **4.75%** across the board — both awards.
- **Body:** Applies to all modern award minimum rates, including **Hospitality MA000009** and **Restaurant MA000119**. Lowest-paid floor: at least **$26.44/hr** (**$1,004.90/wk**).
- **Accent stat (amber, big):** `+4.75%  ·  floor $26.44/hr`
- **VO:** "Tip two — it's 4.75% on every classification in both the Hospitality and Restaurant Awards, with a floor of $26.44 an hour for the lowest-paid."

### Card 3 — Tip 3 (0:15–0:21)
- **Badge:** `3`
- **Title:** **Penalties rise too** — they flow off the base.
- **Body:** Weekend, public holiday, evening, overtime and **25% casual loading** are all multiples of the base rate. Move the base and forget the penalties = every penalised shift is underpaid.
- **Accent example:** `Lvl 2 casual Sunday: ~$58.36 → ~$61.15/hr`
- **VO:** "Tip three — penalty rates and casual loading aren't separate increases. They're calculated off the new base, so they go up automatically — and if you forget them, every weekend shift is underpaid."

### Card 4 — Tip 4 (0:21–0:27)
- **Badge:** `4`
- **Title:** **Re-test your flat & annualised rates.**
- **Body:** A flat or annualised salary that complied last year may now sit **below the Award**. The $26.44 floor can also push the lowest classifications up **more than 4.75%** — don't just multiply. Recompute junior & apprentice dollars too.
- **Accent (red label):** `Don't assume a flat 4.75% on the lowest rates`
- **VO:** "Tip four — re-test every flat rate and salary against the new Award. The ones that just scraped in last year are the ones that fall below now."

### Card 5 — Tip 5 (0:27–0:33)

## The 5 tips (caption-ready, for the post body)

**5 must-know tips on the 2026 Award wage increase 👇**
