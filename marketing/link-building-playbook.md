# Fitz HR — Link-Building Playbook (Zero/Low Budget)

**Goal:** replace the AI-directory-only backlink profile with relevant Australian links, to push the award guides past the position-5 click cliff and re-classify Fitz's entity from "AI tool" to "Australian HR & compliance authority."

**Target:** 10 relevant Australian referring domains in 90 days. That alone would roughly double the quality of the current profile.

**Measurement:** export Bing Webmaster Tools → Backlinks → Referring Domains on the 1st of each month. Success = new domains that are Australian, HR/industry/education-related, or editorial. (Ahrefs referring-domains report as backup.)

---

## The Focus Three (80% of effort goes here)

| Priority | Tactic | Cadence | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | SourceBottle responses | 30 min, twice a week | Free |
| 2 | Resource-page outreach | 1 batch of 10 emails/week | Free |
| 3 | Wage-review media pitch | Once, THIS month (July window) | Free |

Everything else (profiles, chamber, bookkeepers, podcasts) is either one-time setup or a slow-burn pipeline — set up once, then let the Focus Three carry the cadence.

## 30-Day Plan

### Week 1 — One-time setup (half a day total)

> **Constraint: no LinkedIn** (conflict of interest with Blake's current employer — non-negotiable, do not revisit). The entity-signal gap is covered by stacking the alternatives below instead.

- [ ] Create **Google Business Profile** + **Bing Places** for Fitz HR Pty Ltd (strongest free entity anchors after LinkedIn; verify with the ABN address)
- [ ] Activate/claim **X (Twitter) @FitzHR** — the site's twitter:card meta already declares it; an active profile makes it a real entity signal
- [ ] Create **Facebook business page** (low effort, another Meta-verified brand anchor alongside Instagram @fitz.hr)
- [ ] Create **Crunchbase profile** (company-level; founder attribution optional — match whatever visibility Blake is comfortable with)
- [ ] Free listings: **AlternativeTo** (list as alternative to Employment Hero and BrightHR), **SaaSHub**, **Capterra**, **G2**, **GetApp**
- [ ] Sign up to **SourceBottle** (free tier) — set alerts for: Fair Work, wages, small business, hospitality, childcare, NDIS
- [ ] Strengthen the **/about page** as the canonical founder/company bio — without LinkedIn, this page IS the entity hub that journalists and Google check
- [ ] Tell Claude the profile URLs → they get added to the homepage `sameAs` schema and llms.txt

### Week 2 — The July pitch (do not let this window close)
- [ ] Send the **Wage-Review Pitch** (template B) to 6–8 outlets: SmartCompany, HRM Online, Hospitality Magazine, The Shout, The Sector (childcare), an NDIS/community-services outlet, Startup Daily (launch angle), Dynamic Business
- [ ] Personalise the first line per outlet (reference a recent article of theirs); everything else stays templated
- [ ] Start SourceBottle rhythm: respond to 2–3 relevant call-outs this week (template A as the base)

### Week 3 — Resource-page batch #1
- [ ] Build a list of 20 targets: TAFE hospitality/cookery course pages, RSA & food-safety RTOs, bookkeeping course providers, community legal centres, small-business advisory services (state government small business departments often keep link lists)
- [ ] How to find them: search `"useful links" fair work hospitality`, `"resources" award rates site:.edu.au`, `"helpful links" small business site:.org.au`
- [ ] Send 10 outreach emails (template C)
- [ ] Continue SourceBottle (2–3 responses)

### Week 4 — Bookkeeper seeding + batch #2
- [ ] Identify 10 bookkeepers/accountants serving hospitality, childcare or NDIS clients (ICB member directory, LinkedIn search "bookkeeper hospitality Sydney" etc.)
- [ ] Send the **Partner Offer** (template D) — free Pro account for feedback
- [ ] Send resource-page batch #2 (10 emails)
- [ ] Continue SourceBottle
- [ ] Month-end: export Bing referring domains, compare to baseline (9 domains, all AI directories)

### Ongoing monthly rhythm (after day 30)
- SourceBottle: 2–3 responses/week (≈20 min each)
- Resource pages: 10 emails/month
- One media pitch/month tied to a real hook (rate changes, FWC decisions, casual conversion deadlines, penalty-rate news)
- Bookkeeper conversations → feed the Partnerships page when it goes live

## Templates

> Rules for all templates: never invent statistics. Figures come from the FWO Pay Guides / your own published guides. Keep every email under 150 words — recipients skim.

### A) SourceBottle / journalist call-out response

> Subject: [their topic] — founder source with verified award-rate data
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Re your call-out on [topic]: I'm Blake Fitzgerald, founder of Fitz HR, an Australian HR-compliance platform that maintains verified pay-rate data across seven modern awards (hospitality, retail, SCHADS/NDIS, manufacturing, health, children's services), sourced from the Fair Work Ombudsman Pay Guides.
>
> On your question: [2–3 sentences answering their actual question with one specific, checkable figure — e.g. "from 1 July 2026, a casual working Sunday under the Hospitality Award is paid 175% of the ordinary rate — and the most common employer mistake is stacking the 25% casual loading on top when it's already included."]
>
> Happy to provide exact rates for any award/classification, or comment on [their angle]. Can turn quotes around same-day.
>
> Blake Fitzgerald · Founder, Fitz HR · fitzhr.com · [phone]

### B) Wage-review media pitch (July window)

> Subject: The award changes [hospitality venues / childcare centres / NDIS providers] are getting wrong this July — with the numbers
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Loved your piece on [recent article]. With the 2026 Annual Wage Review rates now in effect (1 July), we're seeing the same mistakes repeat across [sector] — and they're the expensive kind, since Fair Work penalties for award non-compliance run to $93,900 per contravention for companies.
>
> Three I can walk your readers through, with current figures:
> 1. Applying the new base rate but forgetting flat-dollar loadings changed too (e.g. hospitality evening loading)
> 2. Stacking the 25% casual loading on penalty rates that already include it
> 3. Missing that each award moved differently — a SCHADS sleepover is not a hospitality split shift
>
> I'm the founder of Fitz HR — we maintain verified rate data across seven awards, updated against the FWO Pay Guides. Happy to supply exact before/after tables for your sector, or a short expert comment.
>
> Blake Fitzgerald · Founder, Fitz HR · fitzhr.com

## Do NOT do
- Paid link packages, PBNs, Fiverr links — poison for a young domain
- More AI-tool directories — deepens the wrong entity classification
- Reciprocal "link exchange" schemes
- Association memberships purely for a directory link (AHA/R&CA can wait until the ROI case is about members, not links)
