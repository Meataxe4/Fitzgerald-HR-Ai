# Fitz HR — Landing Page SEO Review & 5 Complete Page Versions

**Prepared:** July 2026
**Scope:** Review of the current fitzhr.com landing page, followed by five complete, ready-to-build landing page versions — each engineered to rank for one primary keyword and satisfy its search intent, while driving free sign-ups.

---

## Part 1 — Review of the Current Landing Page

### What's working

- **Strong differentiator, clearly stated.** "Award-aware, not generic HR" is repeated consistently (hero eyebrow, awards section, footer). This is the single most defensible claim on the page and the right hill to fight on.
- **Emotional hook in the hero.** "Your chef is a no-show. Fitz tells you exactly what to do." is concrete and visceral — far better than feature-speak.
- **Interactive proof.** The live chat demo and the 8-step Document Builder walkthrough *show* the product instead of describing it. Few competitors do this on a landing page.
- **Real substance in the FAQ.** The penalty-rates answer (Saturday 125%/150%, Sunday 150%/175%, Public Holiday 225%/250% under MA000009) is genuinely useful content that can earn featured snippets.
- **Trust scaffolding.** ABN, Fair Work Act 2009 grounding, FWO Pay Guides, NES, 14-day money-back guarantee, "Built by operators and HR professionals."

### What's holding it back for SEO

1. **The H1 carries zero keyword signal.** "The HR desk every business deserves." is a nice brand line but contains no searchable phrase — no "HR software," no "Australia," no "award," no "AI." The page's one keyword-rich heading ("Award-Aware AI HR & Compliance Software for Australian Business") is buried as the 6th H2, below the fold.
2. **No single primary keyword.** The title tag tries to rank for "AI HR," "Award Compliance Software," and "Australian Business" simultaneously. The page reads as *brand-first*, which is fine for direct traffic but leaves organic queries unclaimed.
3. **Diluted CTA.** The hero offers two CTAs ("Start Free" + "View Pricing"), and later sections add "Try it yourself," "Build your first document free," "Try Fitz HR Free," and "Browse HR Guides." One primary action, repeated, converts better and concentrates internal PageRank.
4. **First 100 words are thin on intent.** After the H1, the visible copy jumps to the no-show scenario without naming the category. Google (and a skimming visitor) has to scroll to learn *what this is*.
5. **The testimonial and stats are under-leveraged.** The Inner West Sydney story is the most persuasive asset on the page and it appears once, mid-page, without a heading. The "$12k avg. annual savings" stat never appears near a CTA.
6. **No H2 answers the money question.** There is no heading shaped like a query real buyers type ("HR software for small business," "Fair Work compliance," "award rate calculator"), so no section can win a snippet for those queries.

### Strategy for the five versions

Each version commits to **one primary keyword**, one search intent, and **one primary CTA** ("Start Free — No Card Required" → sign-up). All statistics, testimonials, pricing and award facts are drawn only from the existing page. Existing internal pages (award guides, blog posts, compare pages, pricing) are used for internal links.
