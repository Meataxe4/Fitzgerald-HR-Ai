When Fitz HR Is the Better Choice

Fitz HR is ideal for day-to-day HR management: answering Award questions, generating compliant documents, managing performance processes, and handling the routine HR issues that make up 90% of a venue's HR needs. It is available 24/7, which matters in hospitality where issues don't happen during business hours. It costs a fraction of what an HR consultant charges, making it accessible to small, independent venues that could never justify a retainer.

When an HR Consultant Is the Better Choice

For complex workplace investigations (sexual harassment, serious misconduct, workers' compensation disputes), representation at the Fair Work Commission, enterprise agreement negotiation, or large-scale organisational restructures, a human HR consultant or employment lawyer is the right call. Fitz HR is not a substitute for legal advice in high-stakes situations.

The Best Approach: Use Both

Most venues that use Fitz HR use it for the 90% of HR tasks that are routine and predictable, and keep a relationship with an HR consultant or employment lawyer for the 10% of situations that are complex or high-risk. This is significantly cheaper than using a consultant for everything, and it means the consultant's time is focused on the matters that genuinely need human expertise.

How Fitz HR Bridges the Gap

Fitz HR includes Crisis Mode, which connects you with a qualified HR professional for urgent situations. Every document generated by the AI can be reviewed by a human HR consultant before you use it. This gives you the speed and availability of AI with the assurance of human expertise when it matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Fitz HR a replacement for an HR consultant?

Fitz HR handles the 90% of HR tasks that are routine — Award questions, document generation, performance management processes. For complex situations like workplace investigations, Fair Work Commission proceedings, or enterprise agreement negotiation, a human HR consultant is still the right choice. Most venues use both: Fitz HR for day-to-day needs and a consultant for complex matters.

How much does an HR consultant cost for hospitality?

HR consultants typically charge $200-$500 per hour or $2,000-$15,000+ per year on retainer. A single warning letter can cost $200-$500. Fitz HR provides unlimited HR guidance and document generation from $249/year, making professional HR support accessible to small venues.

Can Fitz HR provide legal advice?

Fitz HR provides HR guidance and generates compliant documents based on Australian workplace law and the Hospitality Award. It is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. For legal matters, Fitz HR can help you understand the situation and prepare documentation, but you should consult an employment lawyer for legal representation.
